# GATE CS & DA — Exam Pattern & Preparation Strategy

## Exam pattern (both CS and DA papers)
- **Duration:** 3 hours, computer-based test (CBT).
- **Total marks:** 100. **65 questions.**
- **Sections:** General Aptitude (15 marks) + core subject (85 marks).
- **Question types:** MCQ (one correct, negative marking), MSQ (multiple
  select, no negative marking), and NAT (numerical answer type, no negative
  marking).
- **Negative marking (MCQ only):** 1-mark question → −1/3; 2-mark question →
  −2/3. No negative marking for MSQ or NAT.
- **Virtual calculator** is provided on screen.

## High-weight areas
- **GATE CS:** Algorithms, Data Structures, Operating Systems, DBMS, Computer
  Networks, Theory of Computation, Compiler Design, Computer Organisation,
  Digital Logic, Discrete Maths, Engineering Maths, Aptitude.
- **GATE DA:** Probability & Statistics, Linear Algebra, Calculus &
  Optimisation, Programming/DS&A basics, Machine Learning, AI, Database & data
  warehousing, Aptitude.

## Preparation strategy
1. **Know the syllabus & pattern cold** — download the official syllabus first.
2. **Build concepts** from standard sources (NPTEL, standard textbooks),
   then make short formula/concept notes (like the seed notes here).
3. **Practise PYQs relentlessly** — GateOverflow's previous-year question bank
   (CS, DA, plus ISRO/NIELIT/UGC-NET/TIFR) is the single most effective
   resource. Solve topic-wise, then full papers.
4. **Time-bound mock tests** — simulate the 3-hour CBT; analyse mistakes.
5. **Revision cycles** — spaced repetition of formulas, standard results and
   common pitfalls (e.g. Belady's anomaly, pumping lemma, BCNF determinants).
6. **Aptitude & maths are free marks** — don't neglect the 15-mark aptitude
   section; it has high ROI.
