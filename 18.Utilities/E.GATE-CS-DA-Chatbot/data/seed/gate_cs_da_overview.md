## What is GATE?

The **Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE)** is a national-level exam in
India jointly conducted by IISc Bengaluru and seven IITs on behalf of the
National Coordination Board. Scores are used for postgraduate admissions
(M.Tech/MS/PhD) and for recruitment by many public-sector undertakings (PSUs).

Every paper includes a common **General Aptitude (GA)** section worth 15 marks.
The remaining 85 marks cover the subject syllabus. Question types are Multiple
Choice (MCQ), Multiple Select (MSQ), and Numerical Answer Type (NAT). MCQs carry
negative marking; MSQs and NATs do not.

## GATE CS (Computer Science & Information Technology) — syllabus pillars

1. **Engineering Mathematics** — discrete maths, linear algebra, calculus,
   probability & statistics.
2. **Digital Logic** — number systems, combinational & sequential circuits.
3. **Computer Organisation & Architecture** — pipelining, memory hierarchy, I/O.
4. **Programming & Data Structures** — C programming, arrays, stacks, queues,
   linked lists, trees, graphs, hashing.
5. **Algorithms** — searching, sorting, asymptotic analysis, greedy, divide &
   conquer, dynamic programming, graph algorithms.
6. **Theory of Computation** — regular & context-free languages, automata,
   Turing machines, undecidability.
7. **Compiler Design** — lexical analysis, parsing, syntax-directed translation,
   intermediate code, optimisation.
8. **Operating Systems** — processes, threads, CPU scheduling, synchronisation,
   deadlock, memory management, file systems.
9. **Databases** — ER model, relational algebra, SQL, normalisation,
   transactions & concurrency control, indexing.
10. **Computer Networks** — OSI/TCP-IP model, switching, routing, transport,
    application protocols, network security basics.

## GATE DA (Data Science & Artificial Intelligence) — syllabus pillars

(GATE DA is a dedicated paper introduced in 2024.)

1. **Probability & Statistics** — distributions, conditional probability,
   Bayes' theorem, hypothesis testing, confidence intervals.
2. **Linear Algebra** — vector spaces, eigenvalues/eigenvectors, projections,
   SVD, matrix decompositions.
3. **Calculus & Optimisation** — derivatives, gradients, maxima/minima,
   gradient-based optimisation.
4. **Programming, Data Structures & Algorithms** — Python, basic complexity.
5. **Database Management & Warehousing** — SQL, data modelling.
6. **Machine Learning** — supervised (regression, classification, SVM, trees,
   ensembles), unsupervised (clustering, PCA), bias–variance, cross-validation,
   evaluation metrics.
7. **Artificial Intelligence** — search, logic, reasoning under uncertainty.
8. **Deep Learning** — perceptrons, feed-forward networks, back-propagation,
   CNNs, RNNs, regularisation.

## Quick exam-strategy notes

- Master **General Aptitude** and **Engineering Mathematics** first — high
  return on effort across both papers.
- Practise **previous-year questions (PYQs)** under timed conditions; pattern
  recognition matters as much as concepts.
- For NAT questions, watch units and rounding; for MCQs, remember the negative
  marking and avoid blind guessing.
- Maintain a one-page formula/complexity cheat-sheet per subject and revise it
  weekly.
- Track accuracy, not just attempts, in mock tests to find weak topics.
